## Key Issues and Background of the Strike
![Key Issues and Background of the Strike](https://i.ibb.co/TDnJT8Z2/src-0814467e.jpg)
_출처: [thumb.mt.co.kr](https://thumb.mt.co.kr/cdn-cgi/image/f=avif/21/2026/05/2026052116065365651_1.jpg)_
At its core, this strike isn't simply about wage increases — it's a demand to overhaul the entire performance bonus calculation system. The union has strongly pushed for the abolition of the OPI (Overall Performance Incentive) cap and for compensation tied to a fixed percentage of operating profit.
The key issues on the negotiating table can be summarized as follows:
- Abolition of the OPI cap: A demand to remove the existing ceiling on performance bonuses
- 15% of operating profit as bonus pool: A demand for a structure directly linked to company performance
- Additional demands: Work-life balance and labor-management consultation procedures are often raised alongside the main demands
| Category | Union Demand | Management Position |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bonus pool source | 15% of operating profit | Maintain divisional OPI |
| Cap | Abolish | Keep current |
| Payment form | Cash | Partial stock allowed |
Under the tentative agreement on May 20, a new Special Management Performance Bonus was established for the semiconductor division — funded by 10.5% of business performance, with no cap, paid entirely in treasury stock — setting the stage for resolution. However, the union member vote runs from May 22 to 27, and the situation could still flip depending on the result.
> 💡 Key takeaway: This strike isn't a "wage" battle — it's an institutional war over the structure of performance bonuses.
## Shock to the Semiconductor Industry and Supply Chain
![Shock to the Semiconductor Industry and Supply Chain](https://i.ibb.co/sdbDczzk/gen-00220.png)
Because semiconductors rely on a continuous process, even a few hours of downtime delivers a direct blow to yield and quality. It's not uncommon for it to take several days to bring a cooled line back to normal operation.
The loss estimates from the industry and securities analysts are far from trivial:
- Daily revenue disruption: approximately 1 trillion KRW
- Cumulative loss with operating rate down 50%: up to 30 trillion KRW
- Estimated direct operating profit loss: approximately 10 trillion KRW
- Semiconductors' share of Korean exports: roughly 37%
What makes this moment especially critical is that we're in the middle of a supercycle with explosive AI memory demand. With HBM and high-capacity DRAM production ramping up in earnest, any disruption at the Pyeongtaek and Giheung lines could immediately cascade into delays in AI accelerator delivery schedules for global Big Tech players like Nvidia, Google, and Microsoft.
Personally, I think HBM supply chain disruption is the most underestimated risk in this entire situation. Since Samsung and SK Hynix together control roughly two-thirds of the global memory market, even one player wobbling causes market prices themselves to swing.
> 💡 Key takeaway: A prolonged strike isn't a problem for one Korean company — it's a variable affecting the entire global AI supply chain.
## Samsung Electronics Stock Scenarios and Foreign Investor Flows
![Samsung Electronics Stock Scenarios and Foreign Investor Flows](https://i.ibb.co/JRHXXpYZ/gen-00221.png)
The stock has already seen significant volatility. Immediately after the tentative agreement was announced on May 21, Samsung Electronics surged +8.51% to close at 299,500 KRW. It was the result of "removed headwinds" and "a shift to net foreign buying" hitting simultaneously.
Going forward, there are three main possible scenarios:
1. Agreement ratified (base case): Short-term momentum sustained, room for further upside
2. Rejection → strike reignites (bearish): Foreign selling resumes, short-term correction pressure builds
3. Emergency arbitration invoked (wildcard): Short-term rebound, but prolonged labor-management conflict could keep the stock range-bound
| Scenario | Stock Direction | Foreign Flows |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ratified | Up | Net buying continues |
| Rejected | Down | Shift to net selling |
| Emergency arbitration | Volatility↑ | Mixed |
> 💡 Key takeaway: The union member vote result is the first inflection point for short-term stock movement.
## The Supplier Ecosystem and Global Ripple Effects
![The Supplier Ecosystem and Global Ripple Effects](https://i.ibb.co/v61CZB79/gen-00222.png)
When Samsung Electronics alone goes idle, the shock cascades like dominoes: tier-1 suppliers → tier-2 suppliers → the regional economy. Materials, parts, and equipment vendors in the Pyeongtaek, Hwaseong, Giheung, and Cheonan areas depend on Samsung for a substantial share of their revenue.
The areas where the impact is becoming visible can be summarized as follows:
- Delays in etching and deposition equipment orders
- Reduced shipments of materials such as photoresist and CMP slurry
- Declining utilization rates at back-end (OSAT) vendors
- Falling revenue across local commerce (cafeterias, commuter buses, dormitory supply chains)
The global perspective has also shifted quickly. Bloomberg, Reuters, and Nikkei have issued urgent reports on the strike, framing it as "a new risk to the global technology supply chain." With AI and HBM demand surging, major overseas customers and foreign governments alike are closely monitoring Korea's semiconductor supply situation.
> 💡 Key takeaway: The real cost of the strike isn't paid by Samsung — it's paid by thousands of suppliers and global customers together.
## Three Things Investors Should Be Checking Right Now
![Three Things Investors Should Be Checking Right Now](https://i.ibb.co/fV9NHMth/gen-00223.png)
To avoid being whipsawed by news headlines, the safest approach is to track the variables themselves. Here's the checklist I review daily:
- Union member vote results (running May 22–27): Both ratification and rejection rates matter
- Foreign investor net buying trends: Whether daily flows are sustaining a buying tilt
- HBM shipment guidance: Any commentary on supply schedule changes during the quarterly earnings call
> 💡 Key tip: Unless you're day trading, weigh post-event quarterly earnings and HBM shipment volume more heavily.
There's also a pitfall to be wary of. The simple formula "headwind removed = guaranteed upside" is dangerous. Because the +8.5% surge on May 21 already priced in a significant portion of the short-term good news, profit-taking pressure could emerge even if the agreement is ratified.
> 💡 Key takeaway: There are three variables — vote results, foreign flows, and HBM guidance. Tracking just these three is enough.
